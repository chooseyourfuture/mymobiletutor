Outdoors

Nature is highly appreciated in Finland. During winter time people like to go skiing, downhill skiing, skating and ice fishing. In the summer camping, hiking, cycling, swimming, fishing and berry picking are popular leisure time activities. Many Finnish families have summer cottages away from the city to enjoy the silence and nature.

In Finland people have the right to temporarily camp on someone else's land and utilise natural products such as picking berries and mushrooms without the consent of the landowner. This is called 'Everyman's rights' (Jokamiehenoikeudet). Get to know more about your rights and responsibilities concerning the nature here.

Ice fishing and fishing with worm-fishing rod are allowed almost everywhere, yet other fishing requires a permit. Fishing and other wildlife permits can be bought from Eräluvat service.

Sports Park (Urheilupuisto) is an urban park that combines sports and exercise activities, and where the fields and courts of various sports have been situated in a parklike and nowadays even forestlike environment. In the centre of the Sports Park there is the Paavo Nurmi Stadium. A jogging track circles the area. The Sports Park has two playgrounds and several historical buildings.

Castle Park (Linnanpuisto) is the park area that surrounds the historical Turku castle.

Vartiovuori park (Vartiovuorenpuisto) is a popular place in summer to have a picnic, do outdoor exercise, or play games right in the city centre.

Puolala Park (Puolalanpuisto) is one of the most significant constructed environments in Turku City Centre. The architecture and scenery around the park is beautiful. The Puolala Park is situated on a hill only a hundred meters away from the Turku market square.

Kupittaa Park (Kupittaanpuisto) is the largest urban park in Finland, and it is full of different kind of activities. There are a lot of activities, but big green areas as well. There is also a bird park and a childrens traffic park. Kupittaa Park includes the Veritas football stadium, the adventure park, a restaurant, and outdoor swimming arena and the Kupittaa Sports Hall with a lot of sports facilities as well.

Samppalinna park (Samppalinnanpuisto) (webpages only in Finnish) is known for it’s outdoors swimming pool and wonderful views over the city, especially from the place of the Petrelius statue. In addition, it is right near the restaurant ships on the river Aura.

Check out all parks in Turku area.

You can find different kind of trails suitable for cycling, jogging or just walking from Turku website. Trails are usually lightened during 6am to 22pm. Some of the trails around the banks of the River Aura have culture or nature themes.

There are outdoor gym devices within the fitness and jogging trails around Turku area. For example at Kupittaa Park and Sports Park you will find different kind of outdoor gym devices that can be used by everyone.

There are different nature reserves (luonnonsuojelualue) in Turku City area. These areas are very versatile and offer a lot of beautiful Finnish nature scenery.

Kuhankuono area stretches over several municipalities and has many nature trails and beautiful hiking routes. The hiking routes of Kuhankuono are located approximately 35 km north of Turku City Centre. You can take the Föli bus from April to September (buses 21 and 23). You can also check out other buses from Matkahuolto (Turku-Kuhankuono).

Turku Archipelago is considered as one of the most beautiful places around the earth. You can travel around the archipelago by cruises that start from the Turku city cente or by bike if you’re a fitness enthusiast.
One of the closest islands and easiest to get to is Vepsä island. It is an island in Airisto, with a wonderful setting for a day trip. You can get to Vepsä with a water bus from the shore of the River Aura.

Also get to know the Archipelago Trail, which has been chosen as a best Finnish destination.

You can see all the beaches nearby from City of Turku webpages. There are a few by the sea and also a few fresh water beaches. You can reach all these beaches by bus or bike from Turku city centre.

If you prefer something exotic, try winter swimming e.g. in Saaronniemi, Ruissalo.
