Arts and Culture

Finnish culture has its distinct characters as it has been influenced by different styles and trends from both East and West due to belonging under both the Swedish and Russian rules. Finland became independent nation in 1917 and is nowadays know as a well-developed and functional country with many interesting artists, authors and designers. If you know someone who is missing from these pages, we are more than happy to receive feedback.

The Finnish architecture is mostly known for its modernist era. Alvar Aalto is probably the most famous Finnish architect and is often considered to be the most influential of Finnish modernists. Other notable figure of Finnish modernism was Aarne Ervi and the buildings in Turku’s University Hill are of his design during 1956-1959. From architects outside of modernist school the most internationally known are Eliel Saarinen and Eero Saarinen.

Learn more about Finnish architecture from a web journal published by Architecture Information Centre Finland.

Albert Edelfelt, Hugo Simberg, Helene Schjerfbeck and Akseli Gallen-Kallela are famous Finnish painters also known internationally. They were all active during the Golden Age of Finnish national romantic art, spanning some five decades from 1870 to 1920. In addition, Wäinö Aaltonen, born in Turku, is a well-known sculptor who begun his career in the 1920s doing national romantic sculpts. He later experimented cubism and is these days considered to be one of the major Finnish sculptors.

One of the most known still living artist is Kaj Stenvall. His career got under way in 1989 when he started doing stylistic paintings of ‘a very familiar looking duck’. The Disney’s ever popular ducks are usually considered to be the inspiration of Stenvall’s work.

Most well-known Finnish design products are found in the areas of furniture and glass items such as Arabia, Artek, Fiskars, Hackman, Iittala, and Marimekko. The Aalto Vase, also known as the Savoy Vase is probably the most internationally known product of Finnish Design.

Works of e.g. Kaj Franck, Nessi Hovi, Maija Isola, Sanna Kantola, Kalle Kuusela, Stefan Lindfors, Gunnel Nyman, Armi Ratia, Hanna Sarén, Timo Sarpaneva, Nanny Still, Paola Suhonen, Helena Tynell and Björn Weckström are all worth getting to know. In addition there are more and more new and up-and-coming designers entering the Finnish design arena.

Typical Finnish films are either melancholic stories about the darker side of life or light romantic comedies. The master of melancholic movies is Aki Kaurismäki, who was awarded the Grand Prix at the 2002 Cannes Film Festival for the film "The Man without a Past" (Mies vailla menneisyyttä). Other well-known Finnish filmmakers include Renny Harlin, Klaus Härö, Dome Karukoski, Aku Louhimies, Tiina Lymi and Aleksi Mäkelä.

The Unknown Soldier (Tuntematon sotilas) is based on the novel by Väinö Linna (1954) and was re-directed in 2017 becoming the most expensive movie in Finland. The movie is an excellent way to get a glimbse of Finnish history and mentality as it describes the times of The Continuation War between Finland and Russia in 1941-1944.

In Finland, there are many interesting film-related events, such as the Midnight Sun Film Festival held in a small village in Lapland where film directors around the world, up-and-coming talents, the international audience, and the inhabitants of Sodankylä meet.

The annual short film competition Uneton48 is the Finnish equivalent to the international competition The 48 Film Project.

The Finnish national epic Kalevala has gained worldwide reputation. Kalevala partly even inspired the Lord of the Rings trilogy. In fact, J.R.R Tolkien studied Finnish so that he could read Kalevala in its original language. So far Kalevala has been translated into 60 different languages, which makes it the most translated Finnish literary work in history.

The Finnish view of the world is well presented in literary form in the works of e.g. Elias Lönnrot (Kalevala), Aleksis Kivi (the Seven Brothers), Juhani Aho (Railway) and Väinö Linna (the Unknown Soldier). Each one of the mentioned also depict a different era of Finnish history and its transitional stages.

Finnish poetry classically has two names over the others: Finland’s national poet Johan Ludvig Runeberg and Eino Leino. Runeberg’s poem Our Land (Maamme) provided the lyrics for the National anthem of Finland. Leino’s poems have also been converted to musical forms by many of Finland’s most celebrated composers. Of more modern poets internationally known are e.g. Paavo Haavikko, Pentti Saarikoski and Arto Melleri. In addition, the first lady of Finland Jenni Haukio, is a valued poet.

Other Internationally known Finnish writers and novelists, and whose work has been translated to various languages, include F. E. Sillanpää (Nobel prize winner in 1939), Mika Waltari, Arto Paasilinna, Volter Kilpi, Märta Tikkanen, Minna Canth, Bo Carpelan, Sofi Oksanen, Leena Lehtolainen, Kari Hotakainen, Kjell Westö, and Tove Jansson - the creator of Moomins.

Classical music

Jean Sibelius is probably the best known Finn throughout the history of music. His compositions have been played in concert halls all over the world. We highly recommend you to familiarize yourself at least his composition Finlandia. Other important composers, amongst others, are Joonas Kokkonen, Aulis Sallinen, Oskar Merikanto, Toivo Kuula, Leevi Madetoja, Erik Bergman, Osmo Vänskä, Einojuhani Rautavaara and Kaija Saariaho. Of Finnish conductors, Esa-Pekka Salonen, Mikko Franck, Susanna Mälkki, Jukka-Pekka Saraste and Leif Segerstam are internationally recognized conductors. Famous Finnish opera singers include Jorma Hynninen, Karita Mattila, Soile Isokoski and Tommi Hakala. Both Mattila and Hakala have won the BBC Singer of the World in Cardiff.

Rock and pop music

Rock and heavy metal is highly popular in Finland. In fact, Finland has the most heavy metal bands per capita in the world. Most famous bands in this category include e.g. Amorphis, Apocalyptica, Children of Bodom, Hanoi Rocks, HIM, Nightwish, Sonata Arctica, Sentenced, Stratovarius and Stam1na.

Other famous representatives of Finnish pop and rock music are for example Antti Tuisku, Alma, Apulanta, Cheek, Chisu, Elastinen, Isaac Elliot, Jenni Vartiainen, Robin, Sanni, Scandinavian Music Group, Vesala, von Hertzen Brothers and Zen Café. The Finnish hard rock band Lordi won the 2006 Eurovision Song Contest with a record 292 points, giving Finland its first - and only so far - victory in the contest.

Festivals

During the summer, you have various opportunities to participate in music festivals of any genre. For jazz, head to Pori Jazz or Turku Jazz Festival (in March). Ruisrock held in Ruissalo, Turku is one of the most popular festivals in Finland. In addition Provinssirock, Ilosaarirock, Tuska, DBTL in Turku, Qstock, Nummirock Metal Festival, Tammerfest, and many more offer nice way to get to know different parts of Finland while enjoying music and festival atmosphere.

If you're interested in opera, head to Savonlinna Opera Festival. For folk music enthousiasts, check out Kaustinen Folk Music Festival. For a mixture of a bit of everything, experience World Village Festival in Helsinki.

Finland holds the title of most Olympic medals won per capita and has been especially successful in winter sports such as ice hockey, skiing, snowboarding and skating.

Ice hockey is often regarded as Finland’s national sport. It is highly recommended to join at least one hockey game in the stadium or with your friends at home or a bar to experience Finnish enthusiasm towards ice hockey. Finland has won men’s ice hockey world championships in 1995, 2011 and 2019 and practically the whole nation joined the celebrations with the team ‘Leijonat’ (The Lions). Famous ice hockey players are or have been Saku Koivu, Mikko Koivu, Teemu Selänne, Jari Kurri, Olli Jokinen, Tuomo and Jarkko Ruutu, Kimmo Timonen etc. Women’s and juniors’ national ice hockey teams has been highly successful as well.

Finnish baseball is, together with ice hockey, often considered as the other national sport in Finland and is highly popular especially in smaller cities. Superpesis is the national level league played by both men and women.

In addition to ice hockey, Finland is known for motor sports. Famous Finnish Formula 1 drivers are Mika Häkkinen, Kimi Räikkönen, Mika Salo, Heikki Kovalainen and Valtteri Bottas. Famous rally drivers are amongst others Tommi Mäkinen, Juha Kankkunen, Timo Mäkinen, Marcus Grönholm and Jari-Matti Latvala.

Finnish football players such as Jari Litmanen, Sami Hyypiä, Niklas Moisander (Turku), Mikael Forssell and Teemu Pukki among some others are also well known in the European fields. Unfortunately Finland has not yet played at the World Cup (team is called Huuhkajat - Eagle-owls). Local football teams in Turku are FC Inter and FC TPS and you may see these teams at Kupittaa Veritas Stadion.

For nature-loving Finns, orienteering is both a beloved hobby and professional sports. Finland is known for an huge orienteering event called Jukolan viesti, where hundreds of teams, both professionals and amateurs, attend together the same competition. Minna Kauppi is one of the most successful professionals in this field.

Within Finnish sports, the most legendary person is Paavo Nurmi, a famous long distance runner also known as “Flying Finn”. He won 9 gold and 3 silver Olympic medals, and setting altogether 25 world records. In Turku, his home town, there is a statue of him close to the crossing of Hämeenkatu and Kaskenkatu. There is also a sports stadium named after him, Paavo Nurmi Stadium.
